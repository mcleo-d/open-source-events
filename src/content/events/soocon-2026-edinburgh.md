State of Open Con 26 On The Road comes to Edinburgh on 5 June 2026, running 09:30 to 18:00 BST at the Gogarburn Conference Centre. The day climbs the AI stack from compute and national infrastructure through models, agents, identity and security, and finishes on practical questions about data access and return on investment. Tickets include catering on the day and the welcome party on 4 June.

## What's on

The programme opens with Amanda Brock, CEO of OpenUK and OpenHQ, setting out the day. Professor Adrian Jackson, Director of Research at EPCC, The University of Edinburgh, gives the opening keynote on supercomputing and AI in Edinburgh, followed by Nick Jones, VP of Cloud Native Platform Engineering at Nscale, on national AI infrastructure.

A sovereignty panel brings together Camilla de Coverly Veale (Mozilla), Jean-Baptiste Kempf (VideoLAN), Juliette Denny (Growth Engineering) and Sachiko Muto (OpenForum Europe and Drupal). Later, a panel on accessing data in an AI world is chaired across public and private sectors, with Dave Buckley (Open Mined Foundation), Iain Mitchell KC (Scottish Society for Computers & Law), Lee Fulmer (McKinsey), Mallory Durran (Nesta) and Tom Wilkinson, Chief Data Officer at The Scottish Government.

A fireside on The Mythos Effect features Adrian Mouat (Chainguard), Andrew Martin (ControlPlane) and Liz Rice (Isovalent). Expect robot demos from The National Robotarium, two book signings at 12:15 and 15:15, and a live WB-40 Podcast recording.

## Headline speakers

- Amanda Brock, CEO, OpenUK and OpenHQ — host and programme lead for the day.
- Professor Adrian Jackson, Director of Research, EPCC, The University of Edinburgh — supercomputing and AI.
- Anastasia Stasenko, CEO and Co-Founder, Pleias — on the AI commons we haven't yet built.
- Fintan Ryan, Director for Market Insights, GitHub — open source software development under AI.
- Matt Barker, CEO and Co-Founder, BoltMCP — agentic technology and identity.
- Liz Rice, Chief Open Source Officer, Isovalent — fireside on the Mythos Effect.
- Sal Kimmich, CEO, GadflyAI — security and identity for AI teams.
- Tom Wilkinson, Chief Data Officer, The Scottish Government — data access in an AI world.

## Who runs it

State of Open Con is OpenUK's flagship conference brand, and the On The Road editions take it across the UK in 2026 with further stops in Cambridge on 8 July, Bristol, and Belfast on 6 October. OpenUK is a not-for-profit UK organisation for the business of Open Technology, and is a separate body from the Linux Foundation, which runs the Open Source in Finance Forum elsewhere on this site.

## Inclusion and access

OpenUK publishes a [Code of Respect](https://openuk.uk/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/Code-of-respect-Final-Aug-20.pdf) covering conduct at its events. The Gogarburn venue has step-free access and on-site parking. The published programme includes a sovereignty panel and a public-sector data panel that draw speakers from across regulators, civil society and academia.
