The FINOS Open Source AI in Finance Leadership Summit runs on the afternoon of 24 June 2026 in central London. It is an invite-only, closed-door session for senior leaders in financial services, held under the Chatham House Rule and co-located with the Open Source in Finance Forum on 25 June. The format combines keynotes with thematic roundtables, and outputs feed a Strategic Findings Report produced with Linux Foundation Research.

## What's on

The agenda groups three themes. Natalie Daley, Global Head of Cloud Economics and FinOps at HSBC, leads "Balancing Value, Security, and Token Economics" with executives from the FinOps Foundation, looking at how banks measure and control AI spend. David Soria Parra, Member of Technical Staff at Anthropic and co-creator of the Model Context Protocol, covers open source and the agentic future of financial services. Greg Kroah-Hartman, lead maintainer of the Linux Kernel stable branch, presents on AI, cybersecurity and open source, including Project Glasswing and Anthropic Mythos.

Roundtables take each theme into peer discussion, and the day closes with an executive networking dinner.

## Headline speakers

- Natalie Daley, Global Head of Cloud Economics and FinOps, HSBC — value, security and token economics.
- David Soria Parra, Member of Technical Staff, Anthropic — co-creator of the Model Context Protocol, on agentic systems.
- Greg Kroah-Hartman, lead maintainer of the Linux Kernel stable branch — AI, cybersecurity and open source.
- Gabriele Columbro, Executive Director, FINOS — programme host (subject to final agenda).

## Who runs it

The summit is run by FINOS, the Fintech Open Source Foundation, which is a directorate within the Linux Foundation. It is co-located with the Open Source in Finance Forum London on 25 June, also listed on this site, but the two are run as separate programmes with separate registration. State of Open Con 26 On The Road, listed elsewhere here, is unrelated and is run by OpenUK.

## Inclusion and access

The summit is invite-only. Invitations are extended to CTOs, CIOs, Senior Managing Directors and AI leaders at financial institutions. Up to five FINOS member representatives from technology and services vendors may attend as Observers under Steward Sponsorship. Sessions run under the Chatham House Rule, so contributions can be reported without attribution to a specific speaker or organisation. The Strategic Findings Report produced after the event is published openly, so insights from the day are returned to the wider community rather than kept behind the closed door.
