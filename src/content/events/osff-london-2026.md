Open Source in Finance Forum London 2026 runs across 24 and 25 June at a central London venue, with registration opening from 16:00 on 24 June and the main conference on 25 June. The programme is built around what is actually shipping in banks, asset managers and exchanges: AI governance, agentic systems, the Common Domain Model, platform engineering and the OSPO function.

## What's on

Gabriele Columbro, Executive Director of FINOS, opens with the State of Open Source in Finance. Greg Kroah-Hartman, Fellow at The Linux Foundation, follows on AI and the Linux Kernel. Greig Cowan, Head of AI and Data Science Innovation at NatWest Group, and Craig Kitching, Head of the Digital Automation Platform at Fidelity Investments, present industry perspectives on adoption.

On the practical side, Francesco Beltramini (ControlPlane) and William Rizzo (Mirantis) present "Bounded Agent Autonomy, the Cloud Native Way". Tomasz Swierszcz from Citi covers secure open source contributions via the Git-Proxy approach. Dov Katz of Morgan Stanley updates the community on TraderX being ready for business. Ciarán McGonagle (Tokenovate) and David Shone (ISDA) present the CDM Smart Contract Framework, and Mark Paulsen of TD Bank looks at preparing banks for quantum computing risk.

Tracks across the two days include AI Governance and Risk and Security; AI Implementation and Engineering; AI Lightning; Cultivating the Culture (OSPO and Readiness); Fluxnova and Platform Automation; Keynotes; Platform Engineering and Cloud Native; and The Connective Tissue (Interop and CDM).

## Who runs it

OSFF London is organised by the Linux Foundation and programmed by FINOS, the Fintech Open Source Foundation, which is a directorate within the Linux Foundation. OSFF also runs editions in New York and Toronto. The FINOS Open Source AI in Finance Leadership Summit on 24 June is co-located but is a separate, invite-only event run directly by FINOS, listed elsewhere on this site.

## Inclusion and access

The Linux Foundation publishes a [code of conduct](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/open-source-finance-forum-london/attend/code-of-conduct/) and an [inclusion and accessibility page](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/open-source-finance-forum-london/attend/inclusion-accessibility/) covering venue access, dietary needs and quiet space. [Scholarships](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/open-source-finance-forum-london/attend/scholarships/) are available to support attendance for people from under-represented groups in open source. The programme includes a session on "The Emerging FINOS DE Community: Growing Leadership Through Collaboration, Trust and Inclusion" with Peter Thomas (Deutsche Bank), Peter Suggitt, Diana Kennedy and Pooi Cheong (NatWest Group), Mark Brotherwood (Davies Group) and Anthony Kesterton (Red Hat), which is explicitly about how the diversity and equity community in financial services is being built.
